Spoilt Review

I know what you're about to say "really? a Christmas horror movie that got released in cinemas and you really paid money and sat in an empty theatre with overpriced movie snacks to watch it??" and to that I say, I wasn't alone in the theater because I'm an unemployed collage student and i needed my mom to pay; so i didn't even waste money on tickets. THEY WERE NOT A WASTE ANYWAY. All my mentally ill psychiatric patients and personality disorder girlies (gender neutral) will love this one and so will the voices in your head.

I had pretty low expectations because it's a Christmas horror movie and if DVD rental places still existed it seemed like the kind of thing that would be straight to DVD and never see the light of day until your Christian grandma accidentally reads the title wrong and you end up have the best Christmas of your life; if you like horror movies ofc, otherwise it'll just be irritating because you'll have to call an ambulance; unless she's rich and you're included in her will, then it's the best Christmas ever. Anyway, the movie, funnily enough, starts with an old guy in an old age home being struck in the head with an axe. See? I might have ADHD but I always manage to stay relevant. Now most of this review has just been rambling but this movie has, plot, character development and background (with a romantic little side plot that get just the right amount of attention and doesn't distract from the plot (which is apparently hard to do these days)), lore, blood (and even though it came from the people that made Terrifier, it's not a gore-fest) aaaaand you get to see a bunch of Nazi's get massacred and a pedophile killed (two separate incidents but both very satisfying). If that doesn't sell you on this movie then I() don't know why you've read this far and you lowkey just gotta watch it at this point.
